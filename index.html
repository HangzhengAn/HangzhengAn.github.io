Undergraduate related experience

Participation in the Robot World Cup China Tournament

The RoboCup, a highly influential and technologically advanced robotics competition in the field of world robot competitions, was proposed by Professor Alan Mackworth of the University of British Columbia, Canada, in 1992. Its purpose is to provide a landmark and challenging topic for the development of artificial intelligence and intelligent robotics disciplines through robot soccer matches.

During my university years, I participated in the RoboCup China tournament, where I served as a member of the technical team. My main responsibility was conducting training for soccer robot models. This involved adjusting parameters and optimizing methods to facilitate effective coordination among the soccer robots.

Undergraduate academic research

Analysis of the Relationship between Gym Distribution and Infrastructure Based on XGBoost and SHAP

Using Shanghai as an example, after crawling Point of Interest (POI) data with a web crawler, the data is imported into ArcGIS for kernel density analysis, grid processing, city boundary clipping, projection transformation, and coordinate correction. After training the model using XGBoost, SHAP (Shapley Additive exPlanning) is utilized to interpret the model. This allows for the interpretation of the distribution of gyms in the city and analysis of which parts of the infrastructure have the greatest impact on gym distribution

Master's Project experience

Completion of the Autonomous Mobile Robot project, a team collaboration that resulted in a mobile robot capable of autonomous movement and grasping.

Once the robot identifies an object, it automatically navigates to the target location, where a robotic arm accomplishes the autonomous grasping and placement of the object. The main components of the robot include a LeoRover vehicle and a mechanical arm. My primary role involved the recognition of objects suitable for grasping, and the transformation and transmission of their 3D coordinates. The main machine learning recognition method used was YOLO v5.

You can find the full project on GitHub here: Team8 Mobile Grasping Robot.

Master's Study Experience

Development of a virtual version of a floor-cleaning robot.

During my master's studies, I developed a virtual version of a floor-cleaning robot equipped with radar, a depth camera, odometry, and IMU based on specific requirements. The robot can perform differential motion, accept multiple command inputs, but only outputs a single twist message, prioritizing user input.
